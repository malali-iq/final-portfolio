Reflective Introduction

The portfolio I prepared demonstrates substantial growth and an overall overview of the work I've been doing this semester. The initial part of the information is applicable to all racial groupings in the United States. The first project #2 is entirely focused on race, specifically white supremacy. The second project #4 is about gender in our society and its significance, as well as gender's impact on our daily life. The last project #3 is on class in the US and the substantial impact of class and economic variables that take place within this country. All of these projects have been reworked. The original and revised drafts of these can be found at the following URLs. During the revision process, the measures taken were straightforward. I had written and submitted papers that had been graded and on which I had received comments during the semester. I used the helpful input I received to improve these works. I used a lot of the feedback I got to strengthen my paper on these important topics, which I worked really hard on. For some of the primary themes that were presented, I employed a lot of critical thinking as well as thinking outside the box. I was able to successfully construct and enhance my papers to include in this final portfolio as a result of this critical criticism.
